## 12 Lists — daily routines (visual schedules)

**Prioritised by frequency-of-use and earliness in development:**

- Morning routine (your first) — wake, toilet, dress, breakfast, teeth, shoes
- Bedtime routine — bath, pyjamas, teeth, story, lights out
- Brushing teeth — water, toothpaste, brush, rinse, spit, dry
- Handwashing — water on, soap, scrub, rinse, off, dry
- Getting dressed — pants, top, socks, jumper, shoes, coat
- Toilet routine — wee/poo, wipe, flush, wash hands
- Mealtime — sit down, napkin, eat, drink, finished, clear plate
- Bath time — undress, water in, wash, rinse, out, dry, pyjamas
- Going to school — bag, shoes, coat, lunchbox, bye, in the car
- Tidy up time — toys away, books on shelf, cushions back, all done
- Snack time — wash hands, choose snack, sit, eat, finished
- Going to the park — coat, shoes, walk, swings/slide/run, home

That's intentionally domestic-heavy — these are what the parent uses every single day. Special outings (doctor, haircut, supermarket, birthday) are valuable but better as Phase 7 seasonal/situational packs the user can opt into.

## 12 Sentences — pragmatic scripts (templates with slots)

**Organised by communicative function:**

Requesting / refusing (the earliest, most-used functions)

I want ___ — universal request frame
I don't want ___ — refusal frame
More ___ please — continuation request (Banajee's "more" is a top-5 core word)
Can I have ___? — polite request
Regulating (control over environment — hugely empowering)
5. Help me (or I need help) — fixed phrase, no slot
6. All done (or Finished) — closure / escape
7. My turn — social regulation in play

Expressing internal states (the hardest for non-verbal kids — they need scaffolding)
8. I feel ___ — emotion slot (happy, sad, cross, tired, scared)
9. It hurts here ___ — body-part slot (huge for pain communication)
10. I'm hungry (or I'm thirsty) — biological needs

Commenting / joint attention (under-served in AAC starters but foundational)
11. Look at ___ — directing attention
12. I like ___ (or I don't like ___) — opinion / preference

A few framing decisions worth your call
Slots vs fixed phrases: items 5, 6, 10 above are fixed; the rest have slots. Mixing both is realistic — some functions don't need slots.
Yes / No: I left these off the sentence list because they're better as single-symbol responses than sentences. Worth confirming the starter pack already has them prominently in core vocab.
Greetings / manners ("Hello", "Thank you"): also better as core single symbols than sentences. Mention in case you'd rather swap in "Hello, my name is ___" as a 13th sentence for first-meet contexts.
Toileting language: I added it as a list, but you might also want a sentence ("I need the toilet") — it's one of the highest-stakes utterances and worth redundancy.
